The Undersized Superstar?

::: Jalen Brunson was the engine of the Knicks’ historic 2026 championship run, earning NBA Finals MVP honors while leading the postseason in total scoring with 28.4 points per game. In the decisive Game 5, he score 45 points in total and scoring 13 straight in the fourth quarter to rally the Knicks from a double-digit deficit and secure their first title in 53 years. :::

But one phrase in the standfirst of this Wall Street Journal article struck me.

Brunson, an undersized superstar?

According to official NBA figures, Jalen Brunson is 6 ft 2” / 188 cm tall.

In the NBA, where the average player is 200 cm tall, he often appears small. In fact, only 35 NBA players in the 2025-26 NBA regular season are shorter than him and 30 players have the same size.

On the other hand, the vast majority, 89 %, is taller than Brunson.

Stephen Curry, Ja Morant, Trae Young, Tyrese Maxex and 26 other players are also 6 ft 2”.

But among men in the US?

The average standing height is 5 ft 9” or 175 cm.

Which places Brunson in the
96th percentile.

Only 4 % of male adults in the U.S. are taller than him. He is 13 cm taller then the average American male adult.

Remember, at the same time he is 12 cm smaller than the average NBA player.

In Game 5 of the 2026 NBA Finals, he was the shortest player in the Knicks and Spurs starting line-ups.

Yet, he turned out to be the most influential player of the deciding game.
